The book is structured chronologically, covering significant events and movements that shaped the fight for independence. Key themes and topics include: The Revolt of 1857: The book begins with the first major uprising against British rule, analyzing its causes, key players, and the eventual suppression of the revolt. It highlights the role of figures like Mangal Pandey and Rani Lakshmibai, setting the stage for future resistance. Rise of Nationalism: Chandra discusses the emergence of nationalist sentiments in India, focusing on the formation of the Indian National Congress in 1885 and the various factions within the organization, including moderates and extremists.
Gandhi's Leadership: A significant portion of the book is dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi's influence on the independence movement, particularly his strategies of non-violent resistance, including the Non-Cooperation Movement and the Civil Disobedience Movement. Diverse Movements: The authors explore various movements and uprisings, such as the Swadeshi Movement, the Quit India Movement, and the role of revolutionary groups. They emphasize the contributions of leaders like Subhas Chandra Bose and the impact of communal tensions on the struggle. Social and Economic Factors: The book also examines the socio-economic conditions that fueled discontent against British rule, including the exploitation of peasants, workers, and marginalized communities. Partition and Independence: The final chapters address the complexities surrounding the partition of India and the events leading to independence in 1947, analyzing the political negotiations and the consequences of the division.
Chandra's work is noted for its thorough research, drawing on a wide range of primary and secondary sources, including oral histories. The narrative is both analytical and accessible, making it suitable for students and general readers interested in modern Indian history.
