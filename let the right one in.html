Set in 1981 in the Stockholm suburb of Blackeberg, the story centers around 12-year-old Oskar, a lonely and bullied boy who befriends his new neighbor, Eli, a mysterious girl who has recently moved in next door. Despite Eli's strange behavior and the recent spate of gruesome murders in the area, the two form a close bond. As Oskar's friendship with Eli grows, it becomes clear that she is a vampire who has been 12 years old for a very long time. Eli relies on her middle-aged caretaker, Håkan, to procure blood for her. When Håkan's methods become too sloppy and risky, Eli is forced to hunt for herself.
Oskar, unaware of Eli's true nature at first, is drawn to her because she is an outcast like him. He doesn't mind that she only comes out at night and that she has a strange smell. As Oskar learns more about Eli, he must decide if he can accept her for who and what she is. The novel explores themes of bullying, isolation, first love, and the blurring of lines between good and evil. Lindqvist presents a unique take on vampire mythology, portraying Eli as a centuries-old vampire trapped in a child's body. The story culminates in a climactic confrontation as Oskar must choose between his love for Eli and the morally correct thing to do.
